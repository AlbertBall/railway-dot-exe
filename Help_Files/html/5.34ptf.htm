5.3 Headcodes:

Headcodes (i.e. the last four characters in a service reference) must consist of four consecutive alphanumeric characters, i.e. capital or small letters, or digits. The only restriction is that repeating services with increasing digits between repeats (see section 5.7) must contain digits as the last two characters. The standard British headcode consists of 'digit letter digit digit', but other systems use different formats.

See section 5.11 for some important aspects that will help to avoid errors.
